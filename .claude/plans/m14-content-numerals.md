## Citation vs colloquial tone note

- **๑ หนึ่ง** — Wiktionary lists TWO readings:
  - **Standard / citation:** `/nɯŋ˨˩/` = **low** → `/nɯ̀ŋ/` (Paiboon `nʉ̀ng`, RTGS `nueng`). Short vowel `ɯ`, no length mark.
  - **Colloquial:** `/nɯŋ˧/` = **mid** → `/nɯ̄ŋ/`, used in running/informal speech (e.g. fast counting).
  - Recommendation for the listening drill: teach the citation **low** tone `/nɯ̀ŋ/` as canonical; the mid colloquial form is a known variant, not an error.
- All other nine digits have a single citation reading with no standard/colloquial split on Wiktionary.
